6.4.1 Scheduled Export Jobs

Here you can view all your existing jobs to export data. Above the matrix you will find a link Create a new Export Data Job. This link will direct you to the 'Create Scheduled Job: Export Dataset' page. Where you can configure a new job to export your data.
Please fill in the fields named Job Name and Job Description, choose an existing dataset from the drop-down menu and select a period of run. After this, please define the start date and time as well as the format.
The exported file in your choosen format will be emailed to the Email Address provided in the last field.

The Find function, located at the top of the job list, allows you to search for a specific job based on the Name, Previous Fire Time, Next Fire Time, Description, Periode To Run, Dataset, or Study.

The View Scheduled Job matrix consists of the following columns:

Name: This cell indicates the Name of the job.
Previous Fire Time: This cell displays the last date and time the job was done.
Next Fire Time: This cell displays the next time the job will be executed.
Description: The description you typed in during the process of creation of the job, will be listed in this cell.
Period To Run: This cell indicates the period to run the job. It could be daily, weekly, or monthly.
Dataset: Displays the choosen dataset that should be exported.
Study: Indicates the study the job belongs to.
Action: View

Clicking on the View icon will bring you to the View Job page to see all the details associated with this job.

Edit

Clicking on the Edit icon will bring you to the Update Scheduled Job: Export Dataset page where you can edit the job details.

Remove

To remove a job means to pause it by clicking on the Remove icon. The job will remain in the database and can still be restored and modified or deleted at any time. It will not run until it is restored.

Restore

To restore a job click on the Restore icon. Thereafter you can modify the job.

Delete

If you finally want to delete a job you can click on the Delete icon and the job will be wiped from the system, and the delete cannot be undone.
